**Full-screen battery alerts you can't ignore.** Because macOS notifications don't work on your brain.

## Why?

You're deep in hyperfocus mode, crushing it on a project, when suddenly... your laptop dies. The tiny macOS battery warning? You dismissed it without even noticing.

**Your Battery Is Dying** shows a **full-screen alert** that breaks through your focus state before disaster strikes.

Built for:

- People with ADHD who hyperfocus and lose track of everything else
- Anyone who gets "in the zone" and ignores notifications
- Remote workers, students, and creatives who can't afford to lose their work

## Features

- **Full-Screen Alerts** — Impossible-to-miss warnings with glass UI
- **Customizable Threshold** — Set alert level from 1% to 50% (default: 7%)
- **Snooze Option** — "Not now" = remind me in 5 minutes
- **Launch at Login** — Runs quietly in your menu bar
- **Alert Sound** — Optional audio to break through headphones
- **Multi-Display** — Alert shows on all connected screens
- **Zero CPU** — Event-driven monitoring, no polling

### Build from Source

1. Clone the repository
2. Open `deadbatterydummies.xcodeproj` in Xcode
3. Build and run (Cmd+R)

**Requirements:**

- macOS 14.0 (Sonoma) or later
- Xcode 15.0 or later

## Usage

1. Launch the app — it appears as a battery icon in your menu bar
2. Click to access settings
3. Adjust your alert threshold
4. Enable "Launch at Login" for always-on protection

When your battery drops to the threshold:

- A full-screen glass alert appears on all displays
- Click **Dismiss** or press Escape to close
- Click **Snooze** to be reminded in 5 minutes
